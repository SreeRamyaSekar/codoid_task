PERSONAL BANKING

Personal banking refers to a range of financial services and products that are offered by banks and other financial institutions to individual customers. Personal banking services include basic banking services such as deposit accounts, loans, credit cards, and online banking services. Personal banking services are designed to meet the financial needs of individuals, households, and small businesses.

Personal banking products and services are tailored to meet the specific needs of individual customers. For example, deposit accounts such as checking and savings accounts offer customers a safe and convenient way to manage their money. Loans such as mortgages, personal loans, and auto loans offer customers the ability to borrow money for large purchases or unexpected expenses. Credit cards offer customers a convenient way to make purchases and manage their finances.

Personal banking has evolved significantly over the years, with many banks offering a wide range of digital banking services such as online banking, mobile banking, and digital wallets. These services allow customers to access their accounts, pay bills, transfer money, and manage their finances from their computers or mobile devices.

Overall, personal banking plays an important role in helping individuals and households manage their finances and achieve their financial goals.
